Oh man, I thought I wrote one of these already. Here we go then.

Why am I making Chesses 4? Well I’m significantly making it because:

  1. I’m writing a book at the moment about this kind of variation-and-repetition form of design “exercise” and I wanted to make sure I was actually doing it at the same time
  2. In the book I’m talking specifically about chess as an example for the reader to follow along (reasoning that it’s an “easy” game to get up and running in analog mode at least), so it made sense to look at chess again
  3. I had “leftover” chess variations I was reading through as part of working on the book and just the general cheerfulness of the documentation from the previous chesses made me want that feeling again
  4. I don’t feel completely like “myself” if I’m not making a game and this seem the most appropriate and simple game to make

Will it actually help me write my book? Unclear! Hasn’t yet! But it has made me feel more positive about things and that’s well worthwhile.
