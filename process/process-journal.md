# Journal

## A list of ideas (16 June 2026)

Hi diary, I think I want to make more chesses. I've been spending time reading the process repositories for the previous ones and it reminded me of how fun and interesting it is to poke away at chess as a design exercise. I'm writing stuff in my current book about chess and variation and what better way to engage with it a bit more deeply and directly than to make some more chesses of my own? Famous last words? I might prove that I don't know what I'm doing, you say? Mmm!

Anyway - what chesses could I make then. Here's a combination of what's on my mind and stuff I didn't make in previous iterations...

- HALF CHESS. At some point I just wrote that in my Things list of game ideas and chuckled, so I could try to make it. In my mind this involved only the bottom half of a chess board and game, so just the white pieces. And that would be it. Just half of chess. If I wanted to be a real prick about it I could make "THE OTHER HALF" with just the black pieces. Hehehe. Or it could be a premium addition.
- GHOST CHESS. I already made one version of this (as in the movie) but I was struck by how much I enjoyed the strangeness of the more "boring" version where all the pieces would have a lowered alpha and could just move around anywhere, overlapping as many pieces as you like etc. A bit unclear how easy/hard that would be to represent on the underlying board though? Of course a ton of illegal moves, but also dual occupancy of a single square could make using the chess.js representation of the game almost impossible? Or you would have to have a game instance for every single piece maybe... 
- SLOW CHESS. In keeping with my recent game pack. I suppose you just massively slow down the piece movement and that's about it. Could be kind of chill and meditative?
- VOICE RECOGNITION CHESS / ENHANCE. Where it does voice recognition for your moves but then just makes some other random move for you. (Would really really need an AI for this though or it really wouldn't work?)
- EXPLODING CHESS. Pieces with a timer after which they will explode with a blast radius. Seems like it might be tough to communicate all the information involved in that though.
- GRAVES. Captured pieces leave a tombstone and that square can no longer be accessed. Might just get too limited too fast.
- CHESS OF LIFE / CONWAY. Well I've kept wanting to make it so why not want to make it again.
- SCRABBLE. Just the idea of like a TRIPLE WORD SCORE you can get if you land a piece on a specific square. Randomized? Like a pawn can become a bishop if you hit the square or something. And those squares could change over time as well? How quickly does it get too complex/messy?
- MESS. I wrote about this during Chesses 3. The idea that the entire board is just a huge mess and you need to clean it up. But the actual mechanics of that sound tough... what does it mean to tidy up?
- CONFESS. Something about telling a secret with each move. Maybe *I* have to have a confession or something since I'm the only one who can do it in good faith? Maybe there's an occasional "confession square" you could access, but it could be a bad move? Would need an AI (that doesn't give a shit about confessions) to make a player bother to care.
- GUESS. What would you need to guess? Which piece is which? Is there a way it can be like Guess Who, which looks structurally a tiny bit similar? "Can your piece... move diagonally?" and so on. But... that seems unbelievably limited, maybe not enough there for the joke to work.
- LESS. Just fewer pieces. I small little set around the king and you just see what happens? Or just LESS as in you have LESS pieces that your opponent and it becomes unfair?
- BATTLESHIP. Some sort of combined version of those two games? Like a battleship moment and a move-your-pieces moment? Or just a setup moment and then battle ship back and forth? There's maybe something too that but fiddly...?
- CANDY CRUSH. The one Jonathan and I played where if there are three pieces in a line at any moment they all disappear. My recollection is it was a bit stupid but maybe it was of interest as well? Worth trying.
- PLAY IT BY TRUST. Just implementing the Yoko Ono game in chess.js and so on could be kind of interesting? I suppose theoretically needing to make both sets of moves work at all times could be tricky. And it might be nice to be able to play it? But also weird because, well, it's not my game.
- SOME KIND OF PLAY OF PLAY IT BY TRUST? Play it by mistrust? Some version of it that's a fun tweak?
- TROLLEY PROBLEM. Mostly thinking as in you label every piece with a "YOUR MOM" kind of label and then just play chess like that. I guess "YOU" would be the respective kings. Quite funny. (I also wonder whether some other ethical framework could be represented on a chessboard for that matter.)
- SHOVE. No captures by you shove a piece backwards based on the capture rules. Just by one square? Or equal to the amount of "run up" you had. Might be too difficult to do much with the standard starting position. Could you shove a row of pieces rather than just one?
- CHINTZ. I'd hoped CHINTZ had the meaning that CHINTZY has but it doesn't. A sort of "weak chess" would be funny. Like floppy, poorly made pieces, or even the board degrading somehow. But CHINTZ which has the word joke to it, the almost homophone, just refers to the fabric (that came to be thought of as cheap and poor quality)
- RESETS. Every N moves all the remaining pieces reset to starting positions? Some other kind of key moment where all the pieces are reconfigured into a (legal?) position? Maybe even a random configuration of the board for every move? That would be trippy. Fischer random but terrible.
- DEATH SQUARES. Some kind of bomb lands on a square/column after a set number of turns. Any piece there is "captured" and dead? Or even just on a timer... though then you could screw someone over if their piece was there. Maybe it's a bit dull.
- HORRIBLE STORY GENERATOR. Every move is translated into a really shitty story generated via something like Tracery, instead of into chess notation, and you download the story of the game at the end. Once upon a time, a young page strode forth into the world. Kind of thing. Would have to decide what was significant to say for each move etc. Could get overwhelming to make and underwhelming to read.
- UNPOPULAR MOVES. The idea that pieces have feelings about the move and switch sides gradually, changing colour based on their opinion of your approach to the game. Being threatened scares them, say, so at some point you might have to change your play to make them feel better. Though could be really fiddly to indicate what and why is happening.
- PROBABLY CHESS. All the possible squares/moves are given probabilities and you have to decide which piece to move? I feel like we already had the purer idea of that before with just the random move chess.
- FRANKENCHESS. The idea you could move a piece onto another piece and the resulting combined piece could do all the things the two could do, but now it's one piece. So a knight-bishop could make all knight and bishop moves. How would you know you could do this?
- ...

That's a start.
